Matthew Misener Murder Who Killed Him? Where Is He Now?

Publish date: 2024-08-27

Matthew Misener’s Murder was depicted in “Murder in the Wicked West” season 1, episode 3, aptly titled “Murder on Horseshoe Curve.”

Even if every death is awful, the grief and despair surrounding them intensify when it’s unnatural, unnecessary, and completely pointless.

We now have the information you need to learn more about the incident, with a focus on what actually happened, the investigations that followed, and the overall impact.

Matthew Misener’s Cause of Death

As a proud father of three, mining worker, and upstanding local citizen in Safford, Graham County, Arizona, Matthew had established a good life by age 35.

According to court documents, Matthew was ambushed on US Route 191 as he neared the “Horseshoe Curve” between Clifton and Morenci when he was on his way to work at around 5 AM.

In a split second, the driver’s side of his black Chevrolet Tahoe was reportedly hit by 14 gunshots, leading him to lose control and swerve into the side of this off-the-beaten-path highway.

Soon after he was discovered, a bystander called the police, but it was too late; Matt had already passed tragically from his severe injuries.

Who was Matthew Misener’s killer?

The first person the authorities suspected was Matt’s fiance Nicole Chacon since they discovered she had pulled over near the site after becoming aware of the commotion to get back in her car and leave.

At that point, Nicole angrily denied any involvement in the incident. She began to speak openly with the police, admitting she believed Georgina Misener, her love’s ex-wife, might be to blame.

However, even though it appeared that Georgina, who resides in Tuscon, was at home when the shooting occurred, the fact that she was engaged in a custody dispute with Matthew created some questions.

That is how Eduardo Montano Jr. was found and apprehended by the police, only for a subsequent search of his residence to turn up the murder weapon with 14 missing rounds.

The Tucson resident initially denied any involvement in the incident. Still, it soon became apparent that he had lied about practically everything, including his employment, relationship with Matthew, and other details.

Where Are The Killers Now?

While Georgina was detained on February 25 on suspicion of first-degree murder and conspiracy to commit first-degree murder, Eduardo was arrested on February 12, 2020.

However, they didn’t appear in court until later in the year; on September 9, 2021; the former entered a plea of guilty to second-degree murder and conspiracy to commit aggravated assault.

On November 13, Georgina entered a guilty plea to the exact charges. She and Eduardo received consecutive prison terms of 3334 years, divided between 25 years for murder and 834 years for conspiracy. As a result, they have currently incarcerated in Arizona state-run prisons, serving their terms.
