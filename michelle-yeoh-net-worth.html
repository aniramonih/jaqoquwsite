Michelle Yeoh Net Worth: What Is Michelle Yeohs Net Worth?

Publish date: 2024-08-20

Michelle Yeoh net worth-Malaysian actress, Michelle Yeoh Choo Kheng was born on August 6th, 1962 in Ipoh, Perak in Malaysia.

Yeoh was born to a local ethnic Malaysian Chinese family of Hokkien and Cantonese ancestry. Her parents are Janet Yeoh and Yeoh Kian-teik.

Yeoh had a passion for dance from a young age and started learning ballet at the age of four. As a primary student, she attended the all-girls secondary school Main Convent Ipoh in Ipoh.

She relocated to the UK with her parents when she was 15 years old to attend an all-girls boarding school. Yeoh later majored in ballet while attending the Royal Academy of Dance in London, United Kingdom.

She turned to choreography and other forms of art instead of becoming a professional ballet dancer because of a spinal injury. In 1982, she earned a BA in creative arts with a theater minor.

Michelle Yeoh career

Yeoh, being 20 years old, won the Miss Malaysia World pageant in 1983. She represented Malaysia at the Miss World pageant held in London in 1983. She went to Australia later that year and took first place in the Miss International Tourism Quest competition there.

Yeoh began her acting career in action and martial arts movies, where she performed the majority of her own stunts. She received a lead role with her third publication, Yes, Madam (1985). In these previous movies, she was given the name Michelle Khan.

Yeoh returned to acting with Police Story 3: Super Cop (1992) after having divorced Poon. She appeared in The Heroic Trio (1993), and the Yuen Woo-ping films Tai Chi Master and Wing Chun in 1993 and 1994, respectively.

When she began her Hollywood career in 1997 with the film Tomorrow Never Dies, she returned to Michelle Yeoh as her stage name. She played Wai Lin in the 1997 James Bond movie with Pierce Brosnan.

She portrayed Soong Ai-ling in the acclaimed film The Soong Sisters from 1997. Yeoh was asked to play Yu Shu Lien in the Chinese-language martial arts movie Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon by Ang Lee (2000).

Yeoh spoke Malay and English as a child before acquiring Cantonese. She had a little command of M andarin and had phonetically studied the Mandarin dialogue from Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon.

Yeoh founded her own production business, Mythical Films, in 2002 and used it to make The Touch, her debut English-language movie. Yeoh began her English-language career in 2007 with Sunshine after starring as Mameha in the Memoirs of a Geisha adaptation in 2005.

Yeoh co-starred with Brendan Fraser and Jet Li in the fantasy action movie The Mummy: Tomb of the Dragon Emperor in 2008. She played Aung San Suu Kyi in Luc Besson’s 2011 film The Lady.

Yeoh was selected by Guerlain to represent its skincare line in October 2011. Yeoh’s responsibility was to improve ties between the French cosmetics industry and Asia.

Yeoh starred alongside Constance Wu and Henry Golding as the family matriarch Eleanor Young in the 2018 adaption of Kevin Kwan’s novel Crazy Rich Asians by director Jon M. Chu.

Alongside Henry Golding and Emilia Clarke, she portrayed “Santa,” the owner of a Christmas-themed store, in the 2019 film Last Christmas. The movie, which debuted on November 8th, 2019, was a box office hit, earning over $121 million globally.

In the Destin Daniel Cretton-directed Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings movie from Marvel Studios, Yeoh portrayed Ying Nan. The movie premiered on September 3, 2021, in theaters.

Yeoh starred in the science fiction surreal comedy Everything Everywhere All At Once in 2022, which was directed by the Daniels filmmaking team and opened to largely positive reviews in March.

Yeoh will have a role in the upcoming Disney+ series American Born Chinese, which is based on Gene Luen Yang’s book of the same name. She will play the lead in the eight-part Netflix series The Brothers Sun, it was revealed in June 2022.

She will also play Madame Morrible in Jon M. Chu’s two-part film adaptation of the musical Wicked and star in Kenneth Branagh’s A Haunting in Venice.
