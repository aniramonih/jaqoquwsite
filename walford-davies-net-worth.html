FAQs about Walford Davies

Who was Sir Henry Walford Davies?

He was an English composer, organist, and educator known for his significant contributions to British musical life, including his tenure as Master of the King’s Musick from 1934 until his death.

Did Davies contribute to World War efforts?

Yes, during the First World War, he organized concerts for troops in France and musical events for the Fight for Right movement. He was appointed director of music of the Royal Air Force in 1918, where he founded the RAF School of Music and composed the Royal Air Force March Past.

What role did he play in radio broadcasting?

He was a pioneer in radio broadcasting, particularly noted for his BBC programs Music and the Ordinary Listener and Everyman’s Music, which made classical music accessible to a broader audience.
