Monte Cazazza was a one-of-a-kind Talent

Monte Cazazza was a famous American artist, composer, musician, filmmaker, and writer. He is best known for his contributions in helping to shape industrial music through recordings with the London-based Industrial Records in the mid-1970s.

Cazazza was the inventor of the term “Industrial Music” and dedicated his career to the experimental genre. Most of his career was based out of San Francisco where he performed solo and also collaborated with a number of bands.

Some of the bands Cazazza worked with include Factrix, Chaos Of The Night, Psychic TV, The Atom Smashers, The Love Force, and Esperik Glare. He also had a number of memorable live performances including a Marcel Duchamp Club West Show in 1971, and the radical Guerilla Art Activities in 1972.
