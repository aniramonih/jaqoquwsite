Career

Krist Novoselic is a bassist, songwriter, director, author, singer, and Politian- all in one. Though he became known through his rock group Nirvana, Krist has tried his hands at many professions.

In his pre-Nirvana years, Krist would work at a restaurant and later on as an industrial painter. He loved music and would learn to play guitar in his spare time. Kurt Cobain, who was Krist’s brother’s friend, listened to him playing guitar and offered to start a band together.

Krist Novoselic and Kurt Cobain finally started a band with Aaron Burckhard in 1987. Though it was not named as Nirvana initially, it signaled the beginning of the band.

With different drummers, Krist and Kurt released three studio albums-Bleach, Nevermind, and In Utero. Nevermind achieved huge success and went on to become one of the greatest albums of all time.

The Nirvana group disbanded in 1994 following the death of their frontman Kurt Cobain.

Later on, Krist Novoselic created another band named Sweet 75, which released just one album. The group too disbanded in 2000. Krist became involved with other bands like No WTO Combo, Eyes Adrift, and Flipper.

Apart from music Krist Novoselic has also forayed in film direction and acting. He is also active politically and has founded a political committee JAMPAC that is committed to protecting the artist’s rights.

Awards & Achievements

Krist Novoselic received worldwide recognition through his rock band Nirvana. The band won two awards at MTV music awards shows- Best Alternative Video and Best New Artist in a video in 1992. The awards were for their super hit song Smells like teen spirit. The band also won Best alternative video award from MTV in 1994 for their video Heart-shaped box.

Krist also won a Grammy award in 2014 for the song cut me some slack. He performed this song with Paul McCartney, Pat Smear, and David Grohl. The song won in the Best Rock song category.
