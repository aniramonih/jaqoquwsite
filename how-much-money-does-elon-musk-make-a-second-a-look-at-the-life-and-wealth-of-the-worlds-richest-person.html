Elon Musk is a South African-born Canadian-American entrepreneur, inventor, and investor.

He is the founder, CEO, and chief engineer of SpaceX, the CEO and product architect of Tesla, Inc., the owner and CTO of X Corp., the founder of the Boring Company, a co-founder of Neuralink and OpenAI, and the president of the Musk Foundation.

He is also the owner of Twitter, which he acquired in 2022 for $44 billion.

Elon Musk Biography

Musk displayed an early talent for computers and entrepreneurship.

At age 12, he created a video game called Blastar and sold it to a computer magazine for about $500.

In 1988, after obtaining a Canadian passport through his mother, he left South Africa to avoid mandatory military service and seek better opportunities in North America.

Musk attended Queen’s University in Kingston, Ontario, and in 1992 he transferred to the University of Pennsylvania, where he received bachelor’s degrees in physics and economics. He moved to California in 1995 to pursue a PhD in physics at Stanford University, but he dropped out after only two days to start his first company, Zip2. Zip2 was an online city guide that provided maps and business directories to newspapers.

In 1999, Zip2 was acquired by Compaq for $307 million, making Musk a millionaire at age 27.
