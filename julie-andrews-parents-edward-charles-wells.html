Julie Andrews Parents: Edward Charles Wells, Barbara Ward Wells

Publish date: 2024-08-09

Julie Andrews parents, Edward Charles Wells and Barbara Ward Wells, take great pride in their daughter, given her global recognition as a distinguished individual.

Dame Julie Andrews, an acclaimed English performer, boasts a career spanning over 70 years, encompassing stage, film, television, and music.

She appeared as a child actress and singer in London’s West End in 1948 before making her Broadway debut in 1954.

Andrews has garnered numerous awards throughout her illustrious career, including an Academy Award, two Emmy Awards, three Grammy Awards, six Golden Globes, and three Tony Award nominations.

Her remarkable achievements extend to being ranked No. 59 in the BBC’s 2002 poll of the 100 Greatest Britons.

Despite a two-year pandemic delay, Andrews was awarded the esteemed AFI Life Achievement Award in Los Angeles in June 2022. This recognition highlighted her iconic and enduring contributions to the arts and entertainment.

Julie Andrews Parents: Edward Charles And Barbara Ward Wells

Julie was born to her loving parents, Edward and Barbara, in 1935; as of now, 2024, 88 years old.

An acclaimed English performer, Julie’s mother, Barbara Ward Wells, was born in Chertsey in 1910, and the news of her death was confirmed in 1984.

1932, she married Edward Charles “Ted” Wells, a teacher. Julie Andrews was conceived from an affair between Barbara and a family friend.

Barbara told Julie who her birth father was in 1950, although it did not become public until 2008.

As World War II started, Julie Andrews’ parents, Barbara and Ted Wells, separated and divorced.

In 1943, Barbara remarried Ted Andrews. In 1944, Ted Wells married Winifred Maud (Hyde) Birkhead, a war widow and former hair stylist he had worked with during the war.

Julie Andrews family 

Julie Andrews, one of the famous English singers, actresses, and authors, married set designer Tony Walton on May 10, 1959, in Weybridge, Surrey.

The couple first met in 1948 when Andrews performed in the show Humpty Dumpty at the London Casino.

In November 1962, they welcomed their daughter Emma, now known as Emma Walton Hamilton, who later published children’s books. Unfortunately, Andrews and Walton divorced in 1968.

Afterward, Andrews married director Blake Edwards in November 1969. Edwards, who had been her companion for at least two years, directed her in Darling Lili.

She became a stepmother to his children, Jennifer and Geoffrey. In the 1970s, the couple adopted two Vietnamese daughters, Amy (later known as Amelia) Leigh and Joanna Lynne.

They remained married for 41 years until Edwards passed away at 88 on December 15, 2010, in Santa Monica, California. Andrews is now a grandmother to nine and a great-grandmother to three.

Julie Andrews resides in Sag Harbour, New York, where her daughter and co-author, Emma, co-founded the Bay Street Theatre.

Though most of her wealth can be attributed to on-screen work, Andrews has also financially benefited from penning numerous books.
