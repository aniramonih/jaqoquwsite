Early Life

Dame Elizabeth “Liz” Rosemond Taylor, popularly known as “Elizabeth Taylor,” was born February 27, 1932, in London, England. Her father, Francis Lenn Taylor, was an art dealer, while her mother, Sara Sothern, was a stage actress. By birth, Liz had British-American citizenship as her both parents were American.

Liz’s parents moved to London prior to her birth in order to open an art gallery. Liz was born in a very wealthy family and lived a very privileged life in London. Her family had a friend circle of all the rich aristocrats, famous artists, and politicians.

Liz was enrolled in a Byron House, a Montessori School in Highgate. Due to the increasingly tense political situation in England, her family moved back to the United States. Her mother, along with the kids, first moved to Pasadena to stay with Liz’s maternal grandmother, and after a few months, her father joined them.

After a few months, the entire Taylor family moved to Beverly Hills, and Liz joined in Hawthorne School.
