Erin Andrews Bio, Age, Parents, Husband, Children, Net Worth – Erin Andrews is an American sportscaster and television personality. She gained prominence as a correspondent on ESPN in 2004 and later joined Fox Sports in 2012, becoming the lead sideline reporter for the network’s NFL broadcasts.

In addition to her sports career, she participated in ABC’s Dancing with the Stars, finishing third in the tenth season and co-hosting the show from 2014 to 2019 with Tom Bergeron.

Erin Andrews Biography

Born on May 4, 1978, and describing herself as a tomboy in her youth, Andrews was deeply involved in sports, bonding with her father over NBA games, particularly those of the Boston Celtics. Influenced by female sportscasters such as Hannah Storm and Suzy Kolber, she pursued a career in sports journalism.

Andrews attended Bloomingdale High School, where she was part of the dance team, student government, and the National Honor Society. Despite being a tomboy, she participated in dance classes at the Brandon School of Dance Arts in Seffner, Florida. In 1996, she graduated from the University of Florida with a Bachelor of Arts degree in telecommunications.

Her career began in 2000 as a freelance reporter for Fox Sports Florida, and she later covered various sports for the Sunshine Network and Turner South network. Joining ESPN in 2004, Andrews reported on a wide range of sports events, including the College World Series and Major League Baseball.

In 2010, she competed on Dancing with the Stars and finished third. Andrews faced controversy in 2011 over an endorsement deal with Reebok and later became involved in a legal dispute between former ESPN executive Keith Clinkscales and ESPN Vice President Joan Lynch.

In 2012, Andrews left ESPN to join Fox Sports, where she hosted Fox College Football’s studio show and became a contributor to Fox NFL Sunday. She also served as a field reporter for major sporting events.

Aside from her broadcasting career, Erin Andrews engaged in various endorsements and charitable work. She supported campaigns like Kraft Foods Huddle to Fight Hunger, collaborated with StubHub for the Girls Night Out campaign, and hosted the Music Builds: CMT Disaster Relief Concert in 2013. She became the face of CoverGirl at the beginning of the 2014 NFL season and later collaborated with Orangetheory Fitness and Fanatics on different initiatives.
