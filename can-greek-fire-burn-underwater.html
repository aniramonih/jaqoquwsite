What was special about Greek fire?

Developed in the Byzantine empire of the seventh century, Greek fire was a devastating weapon capable of being fired through tubes like a flamethrower, or hurled grenade-style in pots. It stuck to and burned everything, and couldn’t be doused by water, making it especially useful in naval battles.

Is Greek Fire just napalm?

Most modern scholars suspect that Greek Fire was based on a crude or refined type of petroleum—perhaps naphtha, which could easily be found in natural wells around the Black Sea. When mixed with some unknown combination of other ingredients, it makes Greek Fire nothing less than the medieval equivalent of napalm bombs.

Can we make Greek Fire today?

Although the recipe for the true Greek fire, the superweapon used by the Byzantines typically in their naval engagamenets has been lost and therefore cannot be reproduced, Byzantine sources recorded in hand painted images its color. The ingredients used for its manufacture were known only to the Byzantines.

Is it possible for fire to burn on water?

Water is made of hydrogen and oxygen, so it contains both elements that fire needs. However, water is not flammable because the hydrogen and oxygen are already bonded together and cannot react with oxygen any further. Water is actually the end product of burning hydrogen in oxygen.

What are the two types of fire that Cannot be used by water?

Why fire can’t burn water?

A water molecule contains an atom of oxygen and two atoms of hydrogen. It is a completely oxidized substance and thus cannot be oxidized further. Hence water does not burn. Water smothers the fire by creating a barrier between the source of oxygen and the fuel.

What is a Class C fire?

Class C fires involve electrical equipment, such as appliances, wiring, circuit breakers and outlets. Never use water to extinguish class C fires – the risk of electrical shock is far too great! Class C extinguishers do not have a numerical rating. The C classification means the extinguishing agent is non-conductive.

Can there be fire in space?
