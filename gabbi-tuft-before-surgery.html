Gabbi Tuft Before Surgery: The Transformation of a WWE Star

Publish date: 2024-08-11

Gabbi Tuft is a former WWE wrestler who made waves in 2021 when she announced her gender transition. She had gender confirmation surgery and exposed her new appearance to the public.

But who exactly is Gabbi Tuft, and how did she make the transition? Here are some details about her life before and after surgery, as well as her net worth, weight, and height.

Gabbi Tuft  Before Surgery A Successful Wrestling Career

Gabbi Tuft was born Gabe Tuft in San Francisco, California, on November 1, 1978. She was up in Atlanta, Georgia, and was always interested in wrestling. She trained with Mike Bell and made her debut for Ultimate Pro Wrestling in 2007.

In 2008, she joined WWE and was assigned to the Florida Championship Wrestling (FCW) region as Tyler Reks. She won the FCW Florida Heavyweight Championship twice, as well as the FCW Florida Tag Team Championship.

She also appeared on WWE’s main shows, such as Raw, SmackDown, and NXT, as well as in various pay-per-view events, including Summer Slam and the Royal Rumble. She was involved in feuds with wrestlers including John Cena, Randy Orton, and Kofi Kingston.

She left the sport in 2014 to spend more time with her family and pursue other interests. In 2002, she married Priscilla Tuft, with whom she had a daughter, Mia, in 2011. She also worked in marketing and co-founded a fitness website, Body Spartan, with many other wrestlers.

Gabbi Tuft  After Surgery A Brave Coming Out

Gabbi Tuft struggled for years with her gender identity before deciding to transition in 2020. Hormone therapy, facial feminization surgery, breast augmentation, and vaginoplasty were all performed on her. She also updated her official documents and changed her name from Gabe to Gabbi.

She came out as a trans woman in public in February 2021, with the help of her wife and daughter. “This is me,” she said.”I’m proud to be myself.”

She became an advocate for the LGBTQ+ community and documented her experiences on social media and TikTok. She has appeared on various television shows, including Extra, Good Morning America, and Tamron Hall, where she discussed her experiences and problems.

She went on to say, “I hope that by sharing my story, I can inspire others to live their truth and be themselves.”
