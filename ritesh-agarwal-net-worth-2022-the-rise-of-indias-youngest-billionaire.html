Who is Ritesh Agarwal?

Ritesh Agarwal is an Indian entrepreneur and the founder and CEO of OYO Rooms, one of the world’s largest hospitality chains. He is also the youngest self-made billionaire in India and the second-youngest in the world, according to Forbes. He was born on November 16, 1993, in Bissam Cuttack, Odisha, and grew up in a middle-class Marwari family. He was interested in coding and entrepreneurship from a young age and started his first venture, Oravel Stays, in 2011, when he was 17 years old. Oravel Stays was an online platform that offered short-term rentals and bed and breakfasts. He later rebranded it as OYO Rooms in 2013, with a vision to provide affordable and standardized accommodation to travelers across India and beyond.

How did Ritesh Agarwal grow OYO Rooms?

Ritesh Agarwal faced many challenges and rejections in his entrepreneurial journey, but he never gave up on his dream. He dropped out of college and applied for the Thiel Fellowship, a prestigious program that offers $100,000 to young entrepreneurs who want to pursue their ideas. He was one of the 20 fellows selected in 2013 and became the first Indian to receive the fellowship. He used the grant money to expand OYO Rooms and improve its technology and operations. He also learned from mentors like Peter Thiel, Elon Musk, and Mark Zuckerberg. OYO Rooms soon became a disruptor in the Indian hospitality industry, offering standardized rooms with amenities like AC, TV, Wi-Fi, and clean bathrooms at affordable prices. OYO Rooms also partnered with hotel owners and operators and helped them increase their occupancy and revenue. OYO Rooms grew rapidly and expanded to over 800 cities in 80 countries, with more than 43,000 hotels and 1.2 million rooms in its network. OYO Rooms also diversified into segments like co-living, co-working, vacation homes, and cloud kitchens. OYO Rooms became one of the most valuable startups in India and the world, with a valuation of over $10 billion.
