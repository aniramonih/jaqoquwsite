Early Life & Biography

Kamala Devi Harris was born on October 20, 1964, in Oakland, California. Her mother, Shyamala Gopalan, was a Tamil Indian breast-cancer scientist. Her father, Donald Harris, is a Stanford University economics professor. Harris has one younger sister, Maya Harris. She identifies herself as black and Indian; Harris was raised in Berkeley, California.

Harris began kindergarten during the second year of Berkeley’s school desegregation busing program, Harris’ parents divorced when she was seven, and her mother was granted custody of Harris and her sister. After the divorce, Harris and her sister would visit their father in Palo Alto on the weekends.

In 1981 Harris graduated from high school. She enrolled at Howard University in Washington, D.C. She double-majored in economics and political science. In 1989 Harris returned to California, where she received her Juris Doctor (J.D) from the University of California. In 1990 she was admitted to the State Bar of California.

Personal Life

Harris is married to Douglas Emhoff, California attorney. They got married on August 22, 2014, in Santa Barbara, California. They do not have children together, but her husband had children from her previous marriage, namely Cole and Ella. Her sister is Maya Harris, an MSNBC political analyst.

From 1994 to 1995, Harris dated then-California Assembly Speaker Willie Brown. Brown was 30 years older than her and was separated from his wife during their relationship. Harris ended the relationship.

Harris has received honorary degrees from Howard University and from the University of Southern California.

Career

After studying political science and economics B.A in 1986 at Howard University, Kamala earned a law degree in 1989 from Hastings College. She later worked as a deputy district attorney in Oakland from 1990–1998, earning a reputation for toughness as she prosecuted cases of gang violence, drug trafficking, and sexual abuse. She hiked through the ranks and became a district attorney in 2004. In 2010 she was narrowly elected attorney general of California, she won by a margin of less than 1 percent. Therefore she became the first female and the first African American to hold the post.

After taking office, Harris delivered a memorable statement at the Democratic National Convention, raising her national profile in 2012. Harris declared her candidacy, and on the campaign trail, she called for immigration and criminal-justice reforms, increases to the minimum wage, and protection of women’s reproductive rights in early 2015. Harris effortlessly won the 2016 election.

She took office in January 2017, where she began serving on both the Select Committee on Intelligence and the Judiciary Committee and other assignments. Harris published her biography, the Truths We Hold An American Journey, in January 2019. She soon announced that she was pursuing the Democratic presidential nomination in 2020. From the outset, Harris was seen as one of the leading candidates. Until September 2019, when her campaign was in trouble, and in December, she dropped out of the race.
