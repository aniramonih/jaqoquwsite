Early Life & Biography

Shaggy 2 Dope or Joseph William Utsler was born on October 14, 1974, in Wayne, Michigan, U.S. He is of Polish and Cherokee descent. He has two siblings John Utsler who was a member of Insane Clown Posse, and Mike, who is part of the hip hop group Chop Shop. In 2015, his brother John was found dead in his house in Michigan.

When Shaggy was a teenager, he was arrested multiple times. He is said to have suffered from anxiety and depression throughout his career and was once admitted to a drug rehabilitation center to recover.

Career

Shaggy 2 Dope started his musical career in the year 1989 when he formed the group JJ Boys along with Kangol Joe, Joseph Bruce, and John Utsler and released the single “Party at the Top of the Hill.” The following year, they renamed their group as Inner City Posse and performed at local night clubs.

In 1991, they released their debut EP Dog Beats with Psychopathic Records. Shaggy’s brother John Utsler left the group, and the remaining two of them performed hardcore hip hop at various live performances. The group has earned two platinum and five gold albums.

In 1994, Shaggy released his solo music album, Fuck Off! which had four songs. After twelve years, he released his second album F.T.F.O. (Fuck The Fuck Off!) in the year 2006.

In 2017, his third studio album F.T.F.O.M.F. was released, which reached #72 on the Billboard 200, #1 on the Independent Chart, and #36 on the Top Album Sales.

Shaggy also started wrestling professionally in 1983. Along with his brother John and his friend Violent J, he started a promotion named National All-Star Wrestling. They staged their first show, NAW Wrestling Extravaganza, in the presence of family and friends.

In 1996, Shaggy returned to the independent wrestling circuit under the stage name Sewer Dwella. He competed in several independent circuits, including Future Wrestling Alliance and NWA Mid American Wrestling. In 2001, he appeared in XPW Redemption, where he and his tag-team partner defeated Damián 666 and Halloween.

In 1997, he joined Extreme Championship Wrestling and was managed by Paul Heyman. The following year, he appeared in the SummerSlam pay-per-view program of WWF. He wrestled with many popular wrestlers while he was in World Championship Wrestling, including Billy Kidman, Eddie Guerrero, Norman Smiley, Rey Mysterio, Jr., and many more.

He competed in several other promotions, including Juggalo Championship Wrestling (1999–2006), Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2004, 2006), JCW (2007–present), and Limp Bizkit (2018).

Awards & Achievements

Shaggy 2 Dope has won several awards in his illustrious career. He has won the JCW Heavyweight Championship once and JCW Tag Team Championship twice with this tag-team partner Violent J.
