Early Life & Biography

Danny Way, whose birth name is Daniel Way, was born on the 15th of April 1974 in Portland, Oregon, the U.S. to parents, Dennis Way and Mary Way. He has a brother, Damon. His father, Dennis, was jailed for not providing child support to his former wife and, after nine days, hanged himself in his cell. After that, his mother dated several men who abused all three of them. His mother married Tim O’Dea, who introduced Danny and his brother to the world of skateboarding and surfing. The couple eventually divorced, and Tim died after a surfing session.

Career

Danny Way, together with his childhood mentor, Mike Ternasky, founded the skateboard company Plan B in 1991 as a part of the Dwindle Distribution company. The company was re-formed in 2005 with the financial support of Syndrome Distribution. Both of them retained their roles of being the company co-owners and professional skaters.

The company over the years has been sponsored by Independent Truck Company, DC Shoes, Pacific Drive, Nixon, MegaRamp, Capix, and ASEC. In 2005, he became the first and only person to ollie over the Great Wall of China. In 2012, a documentary based on his life was released, which was titled “Waiting For Lightning.” He toured Canada and Australia in support of the documentary. He is a member of the band, “Escalera” as well.
