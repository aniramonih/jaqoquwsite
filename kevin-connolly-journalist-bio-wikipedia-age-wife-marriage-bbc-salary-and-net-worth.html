    Kevin Connolly Wikipedia, Journalist

    Kevin started his career in journalism in January 1994 when he worked at St Petersburg Times as a correspondent. He managed to highlight the decay of the University of South Florida dormitories and long-term plans for expansions and renovations at the Tampa campus.

    In January 1955, he joined The Stuart News as a News Reporter. Here he covered numerous political, policy, and legal battles about growth and development in Martin County. He was part of debates over fresh-water discharges into Indian River Lagoon.

    Later, Kevin joined Orlando Sentinel as a Reporter in September 1977. He Revealed the developer’s attempt to remove a publicly-financed conservation easement on the property in the Volusia County Conservation Corridor. The year 2006 he became an Assistant Editor in the same company.

    In 2007 he has worked as a Volusia County Editor where he directed all news operations spanning two counties, Volusia and Brevard. later in 2008, Kevin was promoted to Deputy Breaking News Editor.

    The year 2014, Kevin became the Content Editor of Breaking News still in Orlando Sentinel where he worked until 2006. He was Directly responsible for weather, traffic, crime, courts, and random chaos in Metro Orlando. He moved to DrugWatch in 2016 August as a Managing Editor.
