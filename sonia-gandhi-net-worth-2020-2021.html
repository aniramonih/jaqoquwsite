She completed her education from the Bell Educational Trust’s Language school in Cambridge City, England, where she received her degree in foreign languages (English and French). She met Rajiv Gandhi at the Varsity Restaurant in Cambridge in 1965, and her father was very much reluctant towards their marriage as Gandhi belonged to a completely different country.

Career

Sonia Gandhi’s political career began after the assassination of her mother-in-law and the active participation of her husband Rajiv Gandhi as the prime minister of the country. She joined politics by joining the Indian National Congress (INC) in 1997, 6 years after the assassination of her husband in 1991. A year later, she became the party leader of the INC.

In 1999, she contested Lok Sabha elections from two places, Amethi, Uttar Pradesh, and Bellary, Karnataka, winning both the seats. She again won in the 2004 Lok Sabha elections from Rae Bareli constituency in Uttar Pradesh. In the same year, she got elected as the leader of a 15 party coalition government named United Province Alliance (UPA). She continued her winning streak from Rae Bareli constituency in successive elections in 2006, 2009, 2014 and 2019.

Throughout her political career, she, as a well-known politician, has been criticized for her involvement in covering up the Bofors Scam. There were also allegations on her for controlling the ex-prime minister Manmohan Singh during his government’s rule, and it was believed that she was acting as the Super PM. Her son-in-law was also allegedly in a real state scam. She was also accused of violating the Income Tax Act of 1961 in 2016.

Awards & Achievements

In 2006, she received the Order of King Leopold, honorary orders of knighthood from the Belgian Government. In the same year, she received an honorary doctorate from Brussels University.
