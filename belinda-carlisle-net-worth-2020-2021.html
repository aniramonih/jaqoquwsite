Early Life & Biography

Belinda was born to Harold Carlisle, and Joanne in Hollywood, Los Angeles on August 17, 1958, her mother was a homemaker and father worked at a gas station. She is the firstborn in the family of seven, and her father abandoned their family when she as just five years old. Carlisle had described her child as not being an easy one and said she was impoverished during her childhood.

Her mother was married again to Wait for Kurczeki, who, according to Carlisle, was an alcoholic. Their family frequently moved before finally settling in Burbank at the age of seven. Carlisle’s interest in music developed at the age of ten.

When she was an adolescent, her family relocated again to Thousand Oaks, California, while there she attended Colina Junior High School and then Newbury Park High School. She was a cheerleader in High School.

Career

Belinda kick-started her music career when she joined the music band Germs as a drummer in 1977. She left the band and co-founded Go-Go with her friends and fellow musicians Elissa Bello, Margot Olavarria, and Elisa Bello. The later left the group and were replaced with new members.

The Go-Go became very successful in the 1980s, they ushered new wave songs into the American Radio and was the first all-female band to ever write their own music and play their own instruments. Some of the albums they released include Beauty and the Beat, Vacation in 1982, and Head Over Heels. They disbanded in 1984.

The Go-Go breakup saw the onset of Carlisle’s solo career, her first Solo album named Belinda was released in 1985 under IRS records, which were the one recording for the Go-Go also. Belinda released albums during her solo career, which included Belinda, Heaven on Earth, Runaway horses, and others.

In 2001, the Go-Go came back together and started releasing songs again. Their first album was God Bless the Go-Gos. Belinda continued solo work also even after reuniting with the Go-Go and released a lot of songs and went on tours to promote their music. Her latest Studio album was released in 2017, which was titled Wilder Shores.
