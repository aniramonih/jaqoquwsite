Early Life

George Walker Bush was born on July 6, 1946, in New Haven, Connecticut, to his parents George H. W. Bush and Barbara Pierce. He grew up in Houston and Midland.

In his school, Bush used to play baseball, while at Yale, he was a member of the rugby team. In 1975, he earned his MBA degree from Harvard Business School. Career

In 1968, George Bush got commissioned in Texas Air National Guard. During his military career, he was assigned to Houston and got discharged in November 1974. In 1977, he established the oil exploration company called Arbusto Energy. This was later changed as Bush Exploration.

After the company got folded with HKN, Inc., he became the board of directors of HKN. Later, Bush became the managing general partner of Texas Rangers for five years. He made an investment of $800 thousand, which later met him receive more than $15 million in 1998.

In 1978, he unsuccessfully ran for the House of Representatives from the 19th congressional district of Texas. He lost to Kent Hance. Later, he became the campaign advisor of his father. In 1994, he became the Republican nominee for the Texas gubernatorial election. He defeated Democratic Ann Richards and became the 46th Governor of Texas.

Bush was re-elected as the Governor in 1998. In June 1999, he announced to run for the President of the United States. In the general election of 2000, Bush defeated Al Gore of the Democratic Party to become the United States President. In 2004, he was again re-elected as the U.S. President by defeating John Kerry of the Democratic Party.

In 2000 and 2004, George Bush was named as the “Person of the Year” by Time magazine. In 2005, he received the Order of the Three Stars from the President of Latvia. He has also received the Order of the Cross of Terra Mariana in 2012 from the President of Estonia. He is the winner of the Air Force Pilot’s Badge. He has also received the National Defense Service Medal, Outstanding Unit Award, and Small Arms Expert Marksmanship Ribbon.
