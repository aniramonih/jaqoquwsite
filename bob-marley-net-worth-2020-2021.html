Early Life

Robert Nesta “Bob” Marley was born on 6 February 1945 in Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica,, to his parents Cedella Booker and Norval Sinclair Marley.

He went to Stepney Primary and Junior High School. He was interested in music from a young age. He was known to be a Rastafarian.

Personal Life

In 1966, Bob Marley got married to Alpharita Constantia “Rita” Anderson. Together they had four children including Cedella, David “Ziggy,” and Stephen. He adopted two children of Anderson from her previous marriage. Marley is known to have a total of 11 children from various women.

In 1977, he developed a malignant melanoma which turned out to be cancer. He died on May 11, 1981, in Miami, Florida. He received a state funeral on May 21, 1981, in Jamaica.

Career

When in Trenchtown, Kingston Bob Marley became a group member with Junior Braithwaite, Beverley Kelso, Peter Tosh, and Bunny Wailer. He became a prominent member of The Wailers. In 1965, they released the album ‘The Wailing Wailers.’

In 1970, he released ‘Soul Rebels.’ A year later, ‘Soul Revelation’ was released. This was followed by ‘The Best of Wailers’. In 1973, they released the album ‘Catch a Fire.’ Another album called ‘Burnin’’ became available from October 1973. A year later, they released the album ‘Natty Dread.’ In 1976, he released ‘Rastaman Vibration.’

Their multi-platinum certified album called ‘Exodus’ was released in June 1977. This was followed by ‘Kaya’ in 1978. In 1979, the group released ‘Survival.’ Their album ‘Uprising’ was released in June 1980. His last studio album was released after his death. ‘Confrontation’ became available from May 1983.

Bob Marley has also released two live albums called ‘Live!’ and ‘Babylon by Bus.’ Their compilation albums include ‘African Herbsman’ and ‘Rasta Revolution.’

Marley has also released many hit singles including “No Woman, No Cry,” “Buffalo Soldier,” “Could You Be Loved,” “Jamming,” “Africa Unite,” “One Love,” “Exodus,” and “Waiting in Vain.”

Awards & Achievements

In 1994, Bob Marley was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. He was conferred with the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 1991. In 1976, his group was named as the Band of the Year by the Rolling Stone. In 1981, he was given the Jamaican Order of Merit. He has also received a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. The United Nations bestowed Marley with the Peace Medal of the Third World in 1978. He has been inducted into the UK Music Hall of Fame.
