Who Are Bling Empire Lynn Ban Parents? Meet Patricia And David!

Publish date: 2024-08-31

Meet Particia and David Ban, the parents of Bling Empire star Lynn Ban. After the spin-off show kicked off in January 2023 in Netflix, her fans were eager to learn more about her family, so sit back and relax. This article covers their age, job, other children, and other additional information.

Delve into this piece to learn more about the Ban family.

Who Are Bling Empire Star Lynn Ban Parents?

Lynn Ban is the daughter of Patricia and David Ban. She was born as the oldest of two siblings to her parents. The family lived in Katong before moving to New York because of her father’s job. She was four then. The family spent three years in New York before moving to Hong Kong in 1979. They returned to New York in 1982 and spent five more years there, after which they returned to Singapore.

Those years away were exciting, Ban recalls, as she often explored the cities with her parents. “We did everything together and there was a sense of curiosity and adventure,” Lynn told The Straits Times.

Meet Patricia Ban, Lynn Ban Mother

Patricia Ban and her daughter Lynn Ban are very close to each other. Pat enjoys having her grandson over. Back in 2015, the three generations vacationed in India. Pat has shared several pictures from their time. Before that, they traveled to Italy. In 2015, the mother-daughter duo traveled to Iceland and then Japan.

On Lynn’s birthday in May 2018, Pat wished her daughter, “Happy birthday Lynn my baby 🎂🌹😘😘😘🎉🎉🎉🎉💐🌷💕💕.”

Patricia also credits her mother for attaining the height of success that she has reached. In fact, she has inherited a sense of style from her mom. Providing a hint of what was to come, her daily path to school was peppered with fashion influences. “My mother would walk me to and from school and we’d take a shortcut though Blooming- dale’s,” she says fondly of the famous fashion emporium.

She recalls shopping there with her mother, as well as the store’s windows and visual displays.

Meet David Ban, Lynn Ban Father

Lynn Ban’s father is a reclusive Singaporean tycoon. David Ban talked fondly about his daughter to The Straits Times in 2014 saying, “Lynn was always determined to do well, whether it was in school or at work. She grew up talking shop with me, so when she showed an entrepreneurial streak, I was more than happy to have her try something different.”

  • David Ban Age

  • David Ban Job

When Lynn Ban was around 4 years old David was working as a bank executive. He was was posted to New York to work for the then-Chase Manhattan Bank. David is currently working as the managing director of Gateway.

David is on the Board of NSL Ltd where he also sits on the audit committee and is the Chairman of the executive committee. He also sits on the Board of Jacob Ballas India Pvt and is a business partner of Mr. Ong Beng Seng since 1980.

He is also the Executive Director of Venus Assets Sdn Bhd which is the developer of the Four Seasons Place project in Kuala Lumpur. The company developed the Four Seasons Place comprising a hotel, service apartments, retail space, and luxury residences.

David talked to SCMP about Malaysia turning into a sanctuary for rich Asians when the project for Four Seasons Place, the tallest hotel/residential building in Kuala Lumpur at 65 floors, took almost two decades to come to fruition.

He said, “We acquired the land in 2003 and didn’t make any plans for it until we were quite sure that the time was right. Kuala Lumpur is entering a new age and there is rising demand for something taller and more spectacular, like a hotel and residence of this calibre. As Kuala Lumpur is also a springboard to some of the most beautiful destinations in the region, we are hopeful that tourist arrivals in the city will increase significantly.”

He is the fourth shareholder who described the gym as his “only heavy lifting these days”. The residences are setting the benchmark for private residences and a 2,700 sq ft loft will cost 9.3 million Malaysian ringgit (US$2.2 million).

David added, “Kuala Lumpur has great potential for the luxury lifestyle market. Besides hotels and residences, we have also brought in famous lifestyle concepts that we feel will do very well. We opened Nobu seeing that the demand for fine dining has increased significantly.”
