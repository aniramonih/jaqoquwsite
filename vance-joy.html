Vance Joy is an Australian singer and songwriter who has gained popularity with his single Riptide, described as a “coming of age love story”. The song was voted number #1 on the 2013 Triple J Hottest 100 as well as became the longest-charting song in ARIA Chart history. His discography also includes other tracks, albums, and extended plays like Dream Your Life Away, Nation of Two, God Loves You When You’re Dancing, From Afar, First Time, Mess Is Mine, Fire and the Flood, Georgia, Straight into Your Arms, We’re Going Home, Like Gold, Lay It on Me, Saturday Sun, Don’t Fade, You (with Benny Blanco and Marshmello), Call If You Need Me, Missing Piece, and I’m with You.

Education

Vance graduated in 2005 from an all-boys primary and secondary Catholic School called St. Kevin’s College in Toorak. He was made school captain while studying there and scored the maximum VCE score of 50 in English at the end of Year 12 of his curriculum.

He went on to acquire a Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Laws from Monash University in Melbourne. Having studied law for 6 years, Vance only needs to complete a one-year-long compulsory traineeship to become a full-time lawyer.

    Vance Joy Facts

  • James came up with the pseudonym Vance Joy after reading a novel named Bliss by Peter Carey.
  • He wanted to debut with his Extended Play God Loves You When You’re Dancing (2013) using a new identity because people often mispronounced his last name ‘Keogh’.
  • After finishing his law degree, Vance gave himself a year to pursue music and started with Open-mic performances at various places in Melbourne.
  • After completing his education, he moved into a small house in Brunswick with a friend and started working as a gardener 3 times a week. Simultaneously, he played in a café in the afternoons introducing himself as Vance Joy to separate his public and private persona.
  • Had his music career not taken off, Vance intended to become a teacher or a librarian.
  • While in college, Vance played Australian Rules Football at the semi-professional level. He was awarded for being the best debut player in 2008 by the Victorian Football League in which he competed for two years in a row as a member of the Coburg Football Club.
  • The hit single Riptide claims Vance was afraid of dentists because he got braces when he was 14 years old which added to his general awkwardness and lack of confidence at that age.
  • He was a fan of Buffy the Vampire Slayer growing up and knows the entire Buffy Musical by heart.
  • Vance is extremely fond of movies and remembers the uncanny amount of background detail about all the movies, he has seen growing up from 1999 to 2014.
  • He accompanied Taylor Swift on ‘The 1989 World Tour’ that started in May 2015 and concluded in Melbourne, Australia in December 2015. Vance was one of the opening acts along with Canadian singer, Shawn Mendes.
  • Vance is slightly self-conscious about his head and lips. He thinks they are too large in proportion to the rest of his body.
  • In 2015, his hit single, Riptide became the longest-running song in Australian charts by staying in ARIA Top 100 for 107 continuous weeks.
  • On February 23, 2018, he released his 2nd studio album titled Nation of Two through Liberation Music. Explaining the album, Vance Joy said, “Nation of Two describes a perfectly self-contained couple; their world beginning and ending at the bed they share, the car they ride in, or any other place where they’re together… the idea that their love for each other gives them their bearings; a point of reference that makes sense of life.”
  • The album Nation of Two peaked at number #1 on the US Folk Albums (Billboard) chart, at number #3 on the US Top Rock Albums (Billboard) chart, and at number #10 on the US Billboard 200 chart. Also, it was certified ‘Platinum’ by Music Canada and ‘Gold’ by the Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA).
