Andra Day- Professional Career

Andra Day had a rocky start to her career, having to work at a variety of odd jobs after graduating from high school. She worked in 20 different occupations, including as a child artist at one of them. Stevie Wonder’s wife noticed Day in 2010, and she received a call from Stevie himself, who introduced her to producer Adrian Gurvitz. In 2011, she got a deal with Buskin Records, and in 2013, Jeffrey Evans became her manager. She got a deal with Warner Bros Records after her covers and YouTube mashups and videos caught people’s attention. Meanwhile, she continued to collaborate with Adrian on over 40 original compositions.

Andra later performed at the Sundance Film Festival for the first time in 2014, and in 2015, she released her debut album “Cheers to Fall,” which was produced by Adrian Gurvitz. Director Spike Lee was impressed by her performance at the Sundance Film Festival, and later filmed her music video for the single “Forever Mine.” She also appeared on Jimmy Kimmel Live, Good Morning America, and other talk shows, performing live at the 2015 BET Awards.

Raise to Fame

Andra Day’s CD set multiple records and received numerous accolades and awards. Her excellent vocals and use of classical jazzy and pop sensations in her music and performances earned her a lot of attention. Her tunes began to appear in television commercials and as background music for performances and other events. She also starred in an Apple TV commercial alongside Stevie Wonder.

Andra had her first-ever world tour in September 2015, and she was also invited to perform her song “Rise” in the White House. She also began forming ties with well-known companies such as McDonald’s and Coca-Cola. She sang at the Democratic National Convention in Philadelphia in 2016 and at the March for Our Lives demonstration in Baltimore with a children’s choir in 2018.

Andra Day began to obtain acting roles as well, and her first was in the film Marshall’s song “Stand up for something” in 2017. In the same year, she voiced the character “sweet tea” in Cars 3, an American animated film. In the year 2021, she landed her breakout role as Billy Holiday in the biographical drama “The United States vs Billy Holiday.” Her performance was lauded and commended, and she was honored with multiple accolades for it.
