Willie Garson is a late American actor and director. Willie Garson  is widely known for playing Stanford Blatch on the HBO series “Sex and the City.”

Early Life

On the 20th of February 1964, Willie Garson was born in Highland Park, New Jersey, in the United States of America. He died at the age of 59 years old and has a birth sign of Aquarius. Garson holds an American nationality and belongs to the Ashkenazi Jewish ethnicity. In addition, he has his belief in Judaism religion and his birth name is William Garson Paszamant.

He is the son of Donald M. Paszamant (father) and Muriel Schwartz (mother). Additionally, he grew up with his two siblings; a brother John Pazamant, and a sister Lisa Clark.

In terms of his education, this man attended Highland Park High School. Later, he enrolled at Wesleyan Univerity and earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in Theater. Additionally, he earned a Master of Fine Arts degree from the Yale Drama School.

Willie Garson – Death

On the 21st of September 2021, Garson died from pancreatic cancer at his home in Los Angeles, California.

Professional Career

Willie Garson began his acting journey in minor roles on shows like “Cheers” (1982), “Family Ties” (1982), “Thirtysomething” (1987), and “L.A.Law” (1986). He appeared in his recurring roles in shows such as “The X-Files” (1993), “Twin Peaks” (1990), and “Two and a Half Men” (2003).

From 1990 to 1995, he was featured in several movies such as “Brain Dead” (1990), “Across the Tracks” (1991), “Ruby” (1992), “Untamed Heart” (1993), “Daybreak” (1993), “Speechless” (1994), and “The Tie That Binds” (1995).

In terms of film appearances, this actor has appeared in three Farrelly Brothers films: “Kingpin” (1996), “There’s Something About Mary” (1998), and “Fever Pitch” (2005). He also appeared in “Fortress 2: Re-Entry”(1999), “Freaky Friday”(2003), “Labor Pains”(2009), “Walk of Shame” (2014), “The Polka King” (2017), and “7 Days to Vegas” (2020). Not only that, but he made a cameo in the end credits of “Jackass Number Two,” with the full context explained in “Jackass 2.5.”

Rose to Fame

He rose to fame after he appeared in a recurring role of Henry Coffield on the police procedural television series “NYPD Blue” from 1996-1999. In addition, he was cast in the recurring role of Stanford Blatch on the romantic television series “Sex and the City” from 1998-2004. Similarly, he appeared as Bruce in the 1989 comedy movie “Troop Beverly Hills.”
