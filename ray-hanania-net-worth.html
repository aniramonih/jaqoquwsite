FAQs about Ray Hanania

Who is Ray Hanania?

Ray Hanania is an American journalist, editor, public relations expert, reporter, stand-up comedian, and a pioneer in Arab American journalism. He has a significant presence in both American and Middle Eastern media, contributing to a variety of publications and hosting radio shows.

What are his main contributions to journalism and comedy?

Hanania has used humor to address and ease the animosity between Jews and Arabs, co-founding The Israeli-Palestinian Comedy Tour. As a journalist, he writes opinion columns that critique government policies in the Middle East, advocate for peace, and challenge extremism. His work spans several awards, including the Sigma Delta Chi National Award for Column Writing.

What stance does he take on Middle East peace?

Hanania has a deep commitment to peace in the Middle East, advocating for a two-state solution and criticizing government policies that hinder this process. He supports Israel’s right to exist and Palestinians’ right to sovereign statehood, opposing violence and terrorism from all sides.

Has he authored any books?

Yes, he has authored several books including ‘I’m Glad I Look Like a Terrorist,’ ‘Arabs of Chicagoland,’ and ‘Yalla! Fight Back: How You Can Stand Up, Speak Out, and Win Against Injustice.’

What challenges has he faced in his career?

Hanania has faced allegations of being pro-Israel, which he denies. Additionally, his career has been marked by challenges such as being dropped from a comedy show due to his Palestinian identity and facing investigation by the FBI due to his activism for Arab American rights.
