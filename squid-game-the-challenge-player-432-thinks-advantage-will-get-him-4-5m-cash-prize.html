Squid Game The Challenge Player 432 thinks advantage will get him $4.5m cash prize Wed 22 Novem

Publish date: 2024-08-06

Two years on from the success of the South Korean Netflix show, the streaming service brings the reality show Squid Game: The Challenge to screens. The reality spin-off show features 456 real players. One of them, Player 432, is a 21-year-old student hoping to bag the cash prize of over $4.5 million.

Packed with the same kind of thrilling action-packed scenes, the show dropped its first batch of episodes on November 22. A whopping 259 players are eliminated in the first game called Red Light, Green Light.

Squid Game: The Challenge – Meet Player 432

A few minutes into Squid Game: The Challenge viewers are introduced to Player 432, Bryton Constantin.

In his opinion, he has the “best number” in the game, explaining: “4,3,2, 1, because I’m the one.”

Bryton hails from South Carolina and at just 21, he’s still in college.

Player 432 is super competitive

Speaking in a confessional on the Netflix show, Player 432 says he thinks he “always will be competitive because Jesus had to compete, that means I have to compete.”

He adds some philosophical insights: “You control your own destiny,” adding that he thinks that “sympathy is a weakness.”

According to Netflix’s Tudum, Bryton is very much interested in “God and the Earth.”

He also enjoys “riding dirtbikes, working out, cooking, and reading.”

Heading into the competition, 432 planned on forming alliances but this had him criticised by some of the other players early on.

He’s super confident and says that he “loves himself.”

Speaking of his personality, Bryton explains: “It’s very confident, it’s very outgoing, it’s almost selfish.”
