Early Life & Biography

Born on March 26, 1976, to Mike Mycoskie and Pam Mycoskie in Arlington, Texas, in the United States, Blake has two siblings, namely Paige Mycoskie and Tyler Mycoskie. Blake went to Arlington Martin High School for his school education and later shifted to St. Stephen’s Episcopal School in Austin in 1995, where he completed his graduation. Blake loved playing tennis and received a partial tennis scholarship in 1995 to attend Southern Methodist University. He had an Achilles tendon injury, which marked an end to his tennis career.

Career

Blake founded an outdoor billboard company during his college, which seemed to be quite profitable as it was bought up by Clear Channel nine days after it’s launch. He went on a vacation in 2006, where he met a lady who was a part of a volunteer organization that provided shoes for children in need. He witnessed various children who could not buy shoes and roam here and their barefooted, which inspired him to create a ‘One On One’ business model which donate a new pair of shoes to the ones who can’t afford it for every new pair sold.

By 2013 his company had donated 10,000,000 pairs to the children in need. In 2011 his company included eyeglasses also as a part of his “One on One” business model. In August 2014, Blake sold 50 percent of his shares to Brain capital, retaining his role as Chief shoe giver. One of his famous sayings is -The more you give, The more you live.
