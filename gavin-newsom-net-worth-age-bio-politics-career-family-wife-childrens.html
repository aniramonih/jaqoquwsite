Early Days in Politics: Laying the Foundation

Gavin Newsom first began his political journey under Mayor Willie Brown of San Francisco during the late ’90s as an appointed member of their Board of Supervisors. Soon thereafter he demonstrated a talent for governance and leadership that quickly brought success for San Francisco as an entire city. This was merely the beginning, as by 2003, the city chose him as its mayor. This marked his ascendancy in the state’s political sphere. The next significant milestone arrived in 2010 when Californians entrusted him with the responsibility of the lieutenant governorship. This trajectory ultimately led him to the gubernatorial office in 2018.
